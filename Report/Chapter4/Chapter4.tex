\noindent The EV contour (Figure \ref{fig:res tp contour}) shows maximum expected value concentrated in regions low profit multiplier $\lambda_p \in [0.8, 1.5]$ and mid-range stop multiplier $\lambda_s \in [2.0, 2.8]$. Most Optuna trials cluster in this region (dense black dots top-left), confirming TPE converged correctly.
\noindent The entropy contour shows entropy rising as profit\_mult increases. The bottom-right region appears to have high entropy; the floor constraint of 0.85 likely cut the low-entropy region. The Max EV optimum at $(\lambda_p=1.25, \lambda_s=2.8)$ sits just inside the valid entropy region, producing an asymmetric label distribution (Table \ref{tab:res tp parameters}) in which the stop-loss class (-1) constitutes only 12.3\% of training labels — less than half the frequency of either other class.

Relative to the balanced configuration ($\lambda_s=1.2$), the Max EV configuration widens the stop-loss barrier more than threefold, reducing the $-1$ label frequency from $33.6\%$ to $12.3\%$ and raising label entropy from $0.9988$ to $0.8921$. Confirmed features (green) characteristics: on-chain metrics (AdrActCnt, CapMVRVCur, CapMrktCurUSD) and medium-horizon momentum indicators (ROC\_30day, ROC\_14day, Momentum\_14day) display the highest median importance scores and widest IQRs, while most technical oscillators (RSI, MACD components, Bollinger Bands) are confirmed but with narrower distributions clustered near the shadow threshold.\\

\subsection{Hyperparameter Tuning}

An Optuna study with 100 trials was conducted for each model, with an early stop after 30 consecutive trials if no improvement in F1-macro was observed. There were no failed runs across any model.

\noindent Across both label configurations (Balanced Entropy and Max EV), the machine learning models consistently demonstrated higher performance on training data compared to the held-out test set, with the XGBoost and Random Forest models exhibiting the largest training accuracies at 0.7231 and 0.8209, respectively. SVC achieved the best test F1 Macro (Balanced: 0.315; Max EV: 0.374), MCC (Balanced: 0.086; Max EV: 0.070), and Balanced Accuracy (Balanced: 0.369; Max EV: 0.401), consistently outperforming all other models on unseen data.\\
In the Balanced Entropy distribution (Table \ref{tab:summary_perf_bal_f1}), the overfit gap was largest for XGBoost (0.4792), followed by Random Forest (0.2922), Logistic Regression (0.2390), and SVC (0.1996); the Stratified baseline recorded almost no overfit gap (-0.0122), consistent with a non-learning model.\\
The Random Forest model under the Max EV objective displayed the most significant divergence between sets, with a training MCC of 0.7096 falling to a test MCC of 0.0549, resulting in an Overfit Gap of 0.6178. Consistent across both experiments, the ROC AUC OVO for all models on the test sets remained near the 0.47–0.56 range, representing a marginal improvement over the random stratified baseline.

\noindent The confusion matrices reveal three distinct failure modes across all eight model–configuration pairs. Under the balanced entropy configuration, Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost each collapse to predicting the stop-loss class for 91–100\% of test observations (Figures \ref{fig:cm_log_bal_f1}, \ref{fig:cm_rf_bal_f1}, \ref{fig:cm_xgb_bal_f1}), despite \texttt{class\_weight=balanced} upweighting minority classes during training. SVC under balanced entropy is the only model to produce non-trivial predictions across all three classes (Figure \ref{fig:cm_svc_bal_f1}), achieving diagonal values of 0.86, 0.06, and 0.18 for the stop-loss, neutral, and profit classes, respectively, though the neutral class recall of 6.1\% indicates the near-linear decision boundary cannot resolve the central region of the feature space.\\
Under the Max EV configuration, Logistic Regression and Random Forest retain the stop-loss collapse (Figures \ref{fig:cm_log_f1}, \ref{fig:cm_rf_f1}), while XGBoost reverses entirely, distributing 82–88\% of predictions between the neutral and profit classes and virtually never predicting stop-loss (Figure \ref{fig:cm_xgb_f1}). SVC under Max EV produces the best confusion matrix in the entire experiment set (Figure \ref{fig:cm_svc_f1}), with diagonal values of 0.48, 0.37, and 0.36, representing the only instance of genuine three-class discrimination across all configurations and directly explaining its superior test F1 Macro of 0.374 and MCC of 0.070.

\noindent Across both label configurations, all models exhibit substantial train-test degradation in every risk-adjusted metric, with some training Sortino ratios an order of magnitude above their test counterparts (Tables \ref{tab:summary_trade_bal_f1}, \ref{tab:sumary_trade_max_ev_f1}). This indicates that train-set trading performance substantially overstates test-set outcomes across all models. Logistic Regression collapses to near-zero trade frequency in both configurations (2 and 4 test trades, respectively), confirming the earlier classification finding that its decision boundary produces almost no actionable signals. The passive buy-and-hold benchmark records a test Sortino of 2.235 under balanced labels and 2.542 under Max EV, providing the threshold above which any model must perform to justify active signal generation over continuous market participation.\\
Under the balanced entropy configuration test set (Table \ref{tab:summary_trade_bal_f1}), Random Forest produces the highest Sortino of 3.543 and the lowest max drawdown of 8.8\% across only 25 trades, demonstrating that a limited number of strategically executed trades can yield substantial returns. SVC and XGBoost both exceed the passive benchmark Sortino of 2.235 at 2.527 and 2.494 respectively, with Calmar ratios of 0.913 and 1.296 against the passive benchmark of 0.947.\\
Under the Max EV configuration (Table \ref{tab:sumary_trade_max_ev_f1}), the train-test Sortino collapse is again universal, with Random Forest falling from 63.315 to 2.787 and XGBoost from 12.439 to 1.709—the only test result below the passive benchmark of 2.542 - whilst SVC degrades from 26.583 to 3.118, retaining the strongest risk-adjusted performance across all configurations. SVC's 279 test trades at 35.4\% frequency produce a Calmar ratio of 1.777 against the passive benchmark of 1.553, whereas XGBoost's higher trade frequency of 45.0\% yields a Calmar of only 0.576 and a max drawdown of 86.7\%, indicating that higher signal frequency does not compensate for the deterioration in trade quality at that density.
