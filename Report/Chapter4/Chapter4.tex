\textcolor{red}{A brief note on class imbalance - max EV label split, stop loss label is a minority across both test and train sets. Less than half of both neutral and profit target labels.
`class weight='balanced'` fixes the *model's response* to imbalance. It cannot fix the *information content* of imbalanced labels. A well-calibrated label set should not need aggressive reweighting — balanced classes are a sign that the barriers are set at a level where the market genuinely splits roughly equally between profit hits, stop hits, and timeouts. That is what Kang (2025) was measuring when he optimised for class balance first.
Justification of chosen values: asymmetric barriers (e.g. profit target > stop loss) reflect positive risk-reward expectations}

\subsection{Hyperparameter Tuning}
Two distinct sets of model hyperparameters were optimised to assess performance across triple-label distributions and study the trade-off between balanced label distribution and optimised for maximum expected value. Optuna study with 100 trials for each model with an early stop of 30 consecutive trials. There were no failed runs across any model.
