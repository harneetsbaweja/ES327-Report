\section{Hardware Implementation}

Following virtual simulation testing, one of the controllers will be evaluated on the physical Quanser rotary pendulum system to assess performance under real-world conditions. Physical effects such as friction, actuator saturation, sensor noise, and unmodelled non-linearities such as the dead-band of the motors, will significantly influence the performance of the controllers in real life. 
\noindent It is worth noting that the gain contributed by the phase lead controller at high-frequencies is less than that of the derivative block in PID, which means that it should be comparatively more robust against noise.

\noindent Ultimately, PID controller might prove to be more robust, given that the Simulink step response of PID has 5\% less overshoot and 1 s less $T_s$ than the phase lead-lag controller.
